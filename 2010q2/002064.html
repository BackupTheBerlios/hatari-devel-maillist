On Wed, 12 May 2010, Vincent Rivière wrote:

> Hello.
>
> In the past, by making some test programs for Windows, I had noticed that 
> requesting a playback at custom replay rate was not always honored, due to 
> hardware limitations. For example, requesting a playback at 12 KHz was 
> leading to an actual playback at 11 KHz, or things like this.
>
> I don't know if this oddity can affect Linux or SDL. But of course, it it was 
> the case, it would lead to delays, etc.
>
> However, 44100 Hz is the big standard, it is supported by all soundcards, so 
> if there are delays in Hatari at that frequency, the problem is certainly 
> unrelated to the issue I describe here.
>
> Just in case...

Hi,

it can happen that the card doesn't support the replay freq. But in the 
case of SDL, the init will fail if the requested freq is not the returned 
freq. Optionnaly, SDL can create a transcoder step to convert from the 
requested rate to the hardware rate in case they differ (but we don't use 
this).

In the case of Hatari, we're guaranteed that the playing rate is the 
requested rate, else sound will be turned off in hatari.

So this rate problem possible too in linux/alsa for example, but it 
shouldn't affect us in sdl..

Nicolas
