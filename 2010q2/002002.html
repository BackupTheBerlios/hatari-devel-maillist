On Friday 07 May 2010, npomarede at corp.free.fr wrote:
> It's true that frameskip can be useful for slower pc/device, but I must
> admit that when I use steem to test some things, I really enjoy the fast
> forward feature, because it really goes fast :)  Much faster than Hatari
> alt+x fast forward mode (using nFrameSkips=0 in my case, because I don't
> want frame to be dropped in the normal case)

With autoskip (frameskip > 4) Hatari doesn't drop frames unless your
computer is too slow.  If it isn't, there's no reason why you would want
frameskip to be forced zero, it will automatically keep at zero until you
use fast-forward.


> Maybe we could keep the fast forward key in Hatari and add a "super fast
> forward" key that would temporarily increase max frameskip to get an
> effect similar to steem (without having to edit config file to
> temporarily change some params and restore them later) ?

You already have that.   Just set high frameskip value.

The limit is small only because high automatic limit could make Hatari
unusable on slow computer.  Frameskip is just a way to to reduce Hatari CPU
load so that it works faster.  After frameskip increases over 4[1], the
screen updates don't anymore have any significant effect on Hatari CPU
usage when it's running at normal speed.


	- Eero

[1] If the automatic frameskip raises over 4 at normal speed, it's a good
     indication that your computer is really to slow for whatever Hatari's
     emulating at that moment.
