Yes, but before new code, FDC timings were notably wrong in most case, a 
side effect of this was that the time out to check a disk is inserted 
was not correct and the floppy part was much faster.

With correct FDC timings, it takes more time for floppy A: to time out. 
I would need to check if there's an easy way to shorten this, but 
changing FDC timings could have negative effects and prevent programs 
that works now from not working anymore again.

Maybe you can press "alt + x" to toggle fast forward mode during boot, 
or start Hatari with an empty disk image inserted in drive A: (using 
--disk-a option) ?
