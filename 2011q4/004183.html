No, absolutely I don't :) Where did you get this? 68030 UM:

moveq #data, Dn 2 0 2(0/0/0) 2(0/1/0)

so for falcon:

moveq #data, Dn 2 0 2(0/0/0) 4(0/1/0)

If there's a moveq.W #<data>, Dn instruction too, I've got the following
> values :
>
1. There's none
2. Even if it were, it's not important. addq.l #data,dn and addq.w #data,dn
will also take the same amount of cycles, you always must look after the
DATA access. In case of moveq, addq, subq, ... the immediate operand is
implicitly encoded into the instruction word, there's no additional bus
activity at all.
