Am Mittwoch, den 21.10.2009, 23:59 +0300 schrieb Eero Tamminen:
> > I actually do not understand why you need a "long long" data type at
> > all....
> 
> To handle both unsigned and signed long values.
> 
> Long longs were already used by conditional breakpoints in bit shift macro
> for this reason.

As a quick fix, I replaced your "long long" with a int64_t from stdint.h
and LLONG etc with INT64_MAX and INT64_MIN respectivly.

I hereby propose to officially switch to the standard for Hatari. The
stdint.h which is part of the C99 standard, defines datatypes with
*known* bit sizes and *known* macro names for such constants.

See http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stdint.h

This header is really a blessing and cures the "how wide is my int data
type" in an efficient and standard way.

I hope you agree so we can merge that sort of fix back and hopefully
refactor all other Hatari parts in the future...
