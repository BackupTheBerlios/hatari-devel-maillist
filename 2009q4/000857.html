> > Is it possible to delay Avi recording starting until bootup has
> > progressed enough that the refresh rate is stable (i.e. to detect that
> > somehow)?
>
> Detecting this doesn't seem straightforward. But recording at 50 Hz
> while atari is at 60 Hz during the boot is not a problem in the end,
> because during the time atari boots at 60 Hz, only a white still image is
> displayed.
>
> So, when playing the avi, you will only see a white image at the
> beginning, and you won't be able to see any problem, because this image
> doesn't "move", so it's not possible to see thata few frames don't have

> screen).

The "problem" I was trying to solve was needing to give the --avi-fps option
in bootup case.  But I guess most people will start it from the GUI anyway
as recording the bootup would just slow down things & waste disk...

Maybe AviRecordDefaultFps should be zero by default and main.c complain
if it's not set (with --avi-fps) when --avirecord is used?

Or --avirecord option itself should require giving the FPS value instead of
there being separate --avi-fps option?
