LAURENT SALLAFRANQUE <laurent.sallafranque at arkea.com> wrote:

> Hello,
> 
> I've noticed an annoying problem with the mouse pointer (this from
> the first time I used hatari with 0.92 release if I'm right).
> 
> In St emulation and windowed mode, the mouse pointer follows
> correctly my moves.
> 
> When in windowed mode, the mouse pointer is "offseted" in falcon mode.
> In falcon mode emulation (no border, zoom st low res), the mouse
> pointer doesn't covers the whole screen.
>
> I have to move the mouse on the left border and on the bottom border
> of the screen to "recalibrate" it.
> 
> It reacts as if the ST borders where taken into account in falcon
> mode to detect the border of the screen.
> 
> I remember somebody complained about this in atari.org forum.
> 
> Anybody detected this ? Any idea on how to correct this annoying bug ?

That's normal, and not a bug. Hatari can not now about the exact
position of the ST mouse pointer, so it's quite impossible to always
sync it with the mouse pointer from the host. Hatari only passes
relative mouse movements to the emulated system, not absolute positions.
To overcome this problem, simply activate the "grab mouse" mode (by
pressing AltGr+m by default).
