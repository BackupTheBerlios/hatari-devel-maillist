> Under most UNIX-like systems, a process can measure user/system time
> spend in it, beside other activity in the system. That is, real
> user/system consumed time, not wallclock time.

I hadn't bothered with that because it requires adding a check to configure,
but now I tested it...

Using times() and only time spent by the Hatari process itself gives *much*
more accurate[1] measurements than SDL_GetTicks() "wall clock" even on
seemingly idle system.  So, I commited that + updated the manual.


	- Eero

[1] the result fluctuation is now ~0.1% instead of earlier tens of percents.
