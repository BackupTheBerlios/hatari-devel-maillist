On Sat, 17 Jan 2009 01:09:51 +0200
Eero Tamminen <eerot at users.berlios.de> wrote:

> > > And with:
> > > $ socat -b1 -v -x PIPE:hatari1-out.pipe PIPE:hatari2-in.pipe
> > > $ socat -b1 -v -x PIPE:hatari2-out.pipe PIPE:hatari1-in.pipe
> > > $ hatari &
> > > $ hatari &
> > > (and connecting the midi I/O appropriately.)
> >
> > That could work, but you then could also use two normal named pipes
> > instead, which you can create with the "mkfifo" command. 
> 
> It's not quite the same.
> 
> From "fifo" manual:
> 	The FIFO must be opened on both ends (reading and writing)
> before data can be  passed.   Normally, opening the FIFO blocks until
> the other end is opened also.
> 
> I.e. The Hatari instances should open the fifos in same order, but
> because both open the output one first, I think they would deadlock.

You're right, it blocks with a deadlock first, but you can overcome
that lock by typing something like "cat /tmp/midi1". Of course you have
then to cancel the "cat" before the emulators exchange any data over
the pipe...
Well, "socat" is certainly the cleaner solution, but the mkfifo trick
is sometimes handy when you're working on a machine where socat has not
been installed.
