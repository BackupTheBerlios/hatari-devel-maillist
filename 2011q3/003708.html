hg bisect is very useful to find where a problem was introuced.

If you know rev A was OK and rev B was bad, but you don't know where the 
change happened, bisect is your friend :)

First mark current rev as bad, for example "hg bisect -b 3420" ; mark 
hatari 1.4 as good "hg bisec -g 2892". Now hg will revert sources to the 
"middle" of those 2 revs. Compile hatari, test and mark the result as 
good or bad with -g or -b (without a revision).

Continue until you find the error, and  hg will tell you what revision 
caused the problem.

So, you should find the cause in O(log(n)), which is quite efficient.
