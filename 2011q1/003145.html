I'm a Junior Contributor to the WahCade project, (a Linux Arcade system front end for multiple gaming systems) and I am trying to increase support and usability of Hatari by WahCade. I have a few observations.

Hatari lacks a "Quick Exit" feature (where one can exit the emulator just by pressing escape.) This makes it very difficult to conform to the orthodoxy of most emulators.

A few feature requests:
Per Axis, per button Joypad configuration. Some arcade based devices have one joypad, and register different controls as the same pad: Example: Player 1: Axis 1 and 2 for Stick, Buttons 0-7 for buttons, Player 2: Axis 3, and 4 for that stick, Buttons 8-15... you get the idea.

Also, the emulator should try and approximate all the screen resolutions X.org allows (for 15 Khz monitors.)

I think you get the idea. These are feature requests.
